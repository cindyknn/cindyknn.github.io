Gene Therapy

​Liệu pháp gen

What is gene therapy?

Gene therapy is a technique which changes the genes in a person’s cells to treat or prevent diseases.

​Scientists are testing several approaches:
  • Replacing a mutated gene with a healthy copy
  • Inactivating a mutated gene that is malfunctioning
  • Introducing a new gene to help treat a disease

How does gene therapy work?

Introduces genetic material into cells to compensate for defective genes/ make beneficial proteins. If a mutated gene causes a faulty/ missing protein, normal copy of the gene introduced to restore the protein’s function.

A gene is inserted into a carrier called a vector, which delivers the gene into a cell. Viruses are often used as vectors because they have the natural ability to infect a cell. They are genetically modified so they can't cause disease in people.

What are the 5 types of viral vectors?

  • Retrovirus integrates its RNA into a chromosome, so new gene is passed on during cell division
  • Lentivirus is a subtype of retroviruses, but can integrate its RNA into non- dividing cells
  • Adenovirus doesn’t integrate its DNA into a chromosome, so new gene isn’t passed onto daughter cells
  • Adeno-associated virus doesn’t integrate its DNA into a chromosome, and is safest for in vivo because of low immune response
  • Herpes simplex virus doesn’t integrate its DNA into a chromosome, and is most effective in targeting neurons
